01. Research

Understanding Designer Pain Points

Direct insights from 3 designer interviews and usability testing with 3 participants, revealing both expected and surprising patterns in feedback challenges.

Research Question

How do designers currently handle design validation, and what specific pain points emerge in their feedback processes that might be addressed through technological solutions?

Interview

Freelance Designer Pain Points

2 out of 3 freelance designers mentioned isolation as their primary challenge, rather than speed of feedback.

"I don't need faster feedback. I need better feedback. When you're working alone, you second-guess everything." - Designer #2
Usability Test

Feedback Quality Issues

During usability testing, participants struggled with vague feedback interpretation, spending more time deciphering comments than implementing changes.

"Getting feedback in 2 hours that says 'make it more modern' is worse than waiting 2 days for specific changes." - Test Participant #2
Analysis

Existing Tool Gaps

Current design feedback tools focus on collaboration workflows but don't address the quality or objectivity of feedback content.

Analysis of Figma, InVision, and Maze revealed workflow optimization but limited validation intelligence.
Insight

Facilitator Observations

As facilitator of 3 usability sessions, observed that users needed structured validation criteria to provide actionable feedback.

"AI can catch accessibility issues, but it can't tell me if my user flow makes emotional sense." - Test Participant #3
